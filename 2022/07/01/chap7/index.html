IV Regression

Consider the regression model, \(Y_t = X_t' \beta^o + \varepsilon_t\). Recall that the consistency of $- ^o 0 $, requires the assumption $E(_t | X_t) =0 $. But, there are three possibility that $E(_t | X_t) $.

  • Model misspecification (e.g. functional form misspecification or omitted variables)
  • Measurement error
  • Simultaneous equation bias

7.2 Assumption

  • Assumption 7.1 [Ergodic stationarity]: \(\left\{ Y_t, \mathop{X_t}\limits_{K\times 1}', \mathop{Z_t}\limits_{l\times 1}' \right\}_{t=1}^{'n}\) is an observable stationary ergodic process, and \(l \geq K\).
  • Assumption 7.2 [Linearity]: $ Y_t = X_t' _{K} + _t$
  • Assumption 7.3 [Nonsigularity]: \(\mathop{Q_{XX}}\limits_{K\times K}=E(X_tX_t')\) is symmetric, finite and nonsingular.
  • Assumption 7.4 [IV conditions]:
      1. \(E(\varepsilon_t|X)\neq 0\)
      1. \(E(\varepsilon_t|Z_t)= 0\)
      1. \(\mathop{Q_{ZZ}}\limits_{l\times l}=E(Z_tZ_t')\) and \(\mathop{Q_{ZX}}\limits_{l \times K}=E(Z_tX_t')\) is finite and full rank
  • Assumption 7.5 [CLT]: \(\frac{1}{\sqrt{n}}\sum_{t=1}^{n}Z_t\varepsilon_t \xrightarrow{d} N(0, V)\) where \(\mathop{V}\limits_{K\times K}\equiv avar(\frac{1}{\sqrt{n}}\sum_{t=1}^{n}Z_t\varepsilon_t)\)

7.3 2SLS Estimation

Since \(E(X_t \varepsilon_t ) \neq 0\), \(\hat \beta\) is not consistent. But if we could decomposes \(X_t\) into two component \(X_t = \tilde X_t + v_t\), where \(\tilde X_t = \gamma' Z_t\) is the population projection, and thus orthogonal to \(\varepsilon_t\). Then, we can still obtain the consistent estimator of \(\beta^o\): \[ \begin{align} Y_t &= X_t' \beta^o + \varepsilon_t \\ &= \tilde X_t' \beta^0 + \underbrace{(v_t' \beta^o + \varepsilon_t)}_{u_t} \end{align} \] Notice \(E(\tilde X_t u_t) = \gamma'E(Z_tu_t)=\gamma'E(Z_tv_t')\beta^o + \gamma'E(Z_t\varepsilon_t)=0\), so this estimator is consistent to \(\beta^o\). But \(\gamma'\) is unknown, \(\tilde X_t\) is unobservable, so we use \(\hat X_t = \hat \gamma' Z_t\) to represent \(\tilde X_t\), where \(\hat \gamma'\) is the OLS estimator of \(X_t\) on \(Z_t\).

Thus, we have the following 2SLS procedures:

  • Stage 1: Estimate the model \[ \mathop{X_t}\limits_{K\times 1} = \mathop{\gamma'}\limits_{K\times l} Z_t + \mathop{v_t}\limits_{K\times 1} \text{ or } X = Z \gamma + v \] And store the fitted value, we have \[ \begin{align*} \hat \gamma &= (Z'Z)^{-1}Z'X \\ \hat X &= Z(Z'Z)^{-1}Z'X \end{align*} \] We actually run \(K\) regressions: \((X_1, \cdots, X_K)=Z(\gamma_1, \cdots, \gamma_K)+(v_1,\cdots, v_K)\).

  • Stage 2: Estimate the model \[ Y_t = \hat X_t' \beta^o + \hat u_t \text{ or } Y = \hat X \beta^o \] we have \[ \begin{align*} \hat \beta_{2SLS} &= (\hat X' \hat X)^{-1}\hat X' Y \\ &= \bigl( X'Z(Z'Z)^{-1}Z'X \bigr)^{-1}X'Z(Z'Z)^{-1}Z'Y \end{align*} \]
