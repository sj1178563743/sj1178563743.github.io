Asymptotic Variance Estimator

To estimate the asymptotic variance $V = \sum_{j=-\infty}^{\infty} \Gamma(j)$, first consider a naive estimator
$$
\hat V = \sum_{j=-(n-1)}^{n-1}\hat\Gamma(j)
$$
where $\hat\Gamma(j) \xrightarrow{p}\Gamma(j)$, but $\hat V \nrightarrow^p V$ since there are too many terms and variance may explosive.

Then, we consider the truncated sum
$$
\hat V = \sum_{j=-p}^{p}\hat\Gamma(j)
$$
But when $p$ is fixed, we could expect $\hat V \xrightarrow{p}\sum_{j=-p}^{p}\Gamma(j) \neq V$.

Hence, we should let $p$ grows to infinity as $n \rightarrow \infty$ but cannot grow as fast as sample size $n$. Otherwise, the variance of $\hat V$ will never vanish to zero. Therefore, to ensure consistency of $\hat V$ to $V$, we should balance the bias and the vaiance of $\hat V$ properly. Consider
$$
\hat V = \sum_{j=-p_n}^{p_n}\hat\Gamma(j), \text{ where } p_n \rightarrow \infty, p_n/n \rightarrow 0
$$
Although this estimator is consistent for $V$, it may not be positive semi-definite for all $n$. So, use a weighted average estimator where weighting function $k(j/p_n)$ is kernel function.
$$
\hat V = \sum_{j=-p_n}^{p_n}k(j/p_n)\hat\Gamma(j)
$$
